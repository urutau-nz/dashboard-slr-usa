Achieving X-Minute Cities

As cities rapidly urbanise and adapt to climate change, there is an increasing need to shift the design of built environments to be human-centric and easy to traverse. Introducing: The X-Minute City, where residents are within X minutes of amenities via walking and cycling. Communities with high proximity to amenities creates a vibrant and diverse city.

This tool enables local government, central government, and communities to:

  • Guide investment prioritisation in creating highly accessible cities through place making and smart amenity placement
  • Guide the densification and urban renaissance of towns and cities in Aotearoa
  • Identify neighbourhoods that are underserved by a range of amenities
  • Identify the inequities of city design by income, ethnicity and disability
  • Adapt to and mitigate climate change through promoting active modes of transit, such as walking, cycling and using a wheelchair
